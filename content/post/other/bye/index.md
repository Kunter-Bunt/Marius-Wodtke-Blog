You might have noticed that yesterday three articles arrived instead on the just one. That has a specific reason: I am no longer Microsoft MVP and this will ultimately mean that I will radically change how I spent my time. 

Apparently, the contribution year was not strong enough:
- 14 Blog posts (https://www.marius-wodtke.de/)
- 12 Videos on my channel -> Of which 2 main ideas "Calling Custom APIs" and "Client API" were recently implemented by MS as native Power Pages capabilities. (https://www.youtube.com/@EverythingAroundDataverse)
- 12 Videos on the Power Platform Learners channel with me as speaker (https://www.youtube.com/@PowerPlatformLearners)
- Co-Organizing and Speaking at Power Platform Learners Community, the Power Platform Advent Calendar and Global Power Platform Bootcamp Berlin
- Speaking at Power Summit, a German conference
- Publishing an XrmToolbox Tool (Teams Integration Enabler)
- And as I am a developer, these also mean maintaining half-dozen repositories with samples and ready-made tools. (https://github.com/Kunter-Bunt)

I doubt that Microsoft sees a lack of volume or cadence here, it has to be something else, like receiving the content as not valuable. Yet, I was unable to get feedback about the reasons. 
I liked what I was doing in the community, but without concrete reasons I do not see getting this recognition back. 

And this sadly puts me into a bad situation, companies like my employer are always drawn apart over public sharing of knowledge and the lack of support from Microsoft hits my ambitions quite a bit. Now I even have trouble justifying the time I spend to prepare stuff to myself. 

And with that insight, can I use my time better? I will certainly try! But this means cutting off what I was doing until now, so expect much less appearances in the future months. I will start finding a new mode, maybe that will not include public appearances at all, maybe I will explore the space of tool creation. Who knows.

Thanks to the people taking this journey with me like @Ambesh @Jörgen @Bülent @Jayshree @Reshmee @Thomas @Milad @Sweta @Michael, to all our guests in GBBP and Adventcalendar, to everyone I recently met at EPPC and to all the people with the encouraging words that held me up!

Content will stay online for now, I hope you enjoyed my recent contributions like the speaking at EPPC, the portation of the Teams Integration Enabler for the new Power Platform Toolbox and my series on Managed Identities for Dataverse. You can use https://www.marius-wodtke.de/ as the hub to find everything.

It's still summer, so I'm off to enjoying the sun now, see you around!
